Welcome

    The Little Monster in Your Closet (2018)
    Roles: Project Lead, Creative Lead, Programmer, Level Designer
    Team Size: 9 people
    1 / 3
    You play as a monster in a human child's bedroom. Your objective is to make as big of a mess and as much noise as possible before time runs out.
    2 / 3
    Knocking objects over rewards the player with points and increments their score multiplier. There are objects to be knocked over all over the room!
    3 / 3
    Balloons let the player keep their combo going and serve as small rewards while going from one point to another. When the time is up, the player's rank is calculated based on the number of star milestones they reached in the score counter.
    ❮ ❯

    The Little Monster in Your Closet is a 3D Platformer in which players have to spit, bump and pop various objects in order to make a lot of noise and leave levels in a state of chaos. Players can perform double jumps and dashes in order to get around quickly. The game features one level and was developed over the course of five months.

    As the project lead and main programmer I had my hands on various parts of this game's development. I was the team member most closely connected to the game and made a lot of creative decisions with the goal of improving it and meeting the deadline.
    System Sync (2018)
    Roles: Project Lead, Artist, Programmer, Level Designer
    Team size: 3 people
    1 / 3
    The goal of each level is to activate all modules connected to the central device simultaneously. The title screen teaches this with coloured buttons, which players have to aim at. Players can also use the title screen to experiment with the game's controls.
    2 / 3
    Teamwork is essential. Here, one player has to help the other cross a large gap. Since both players are positively charged, one can lift the other to prevent them from falling into the yellow death ray.
    3 / 3
    We built levels by using a tilemap for the terrain. Once the terrain was mostly final we added and linked objects such as doors and switches to the levels. This allowed us to prototype and adjust levels and individual challenges in a matter of minutes.
    ❮ ❯

    System Sync is a 2D Co-op Puzzle Platformer in which players have to roll, jump and lift each other in order to overcome magnetism-based puzzles. Players can switch between ball and turret states, depending on which they can either jump or fire a magnetic beam. The game features four levels and was developed over the course of five months.

    As the project lead and main programmer I had my hands on various parts of this game's development. I programmed most of the game, designed nearly every scene, did a lot of sound design and gave creative direction and feedback when necessary.
    A Christmas Thing (2017)
    Roles: Artist, Programmer, Level Designer
    Team size: 3 people
    1 / 3
    Demo in the game's hub, 'Popsicle Garden'. Next to her is a teleporter to one of the stages.
    2 / 3
    Showcase of 'Candy Cove', one of the levels I designed. The player start point is on the platform to the right. The level is designed with 4-step level design in mind and is built around the red and white platforms which spin in various ways.
    3 / 3
    Showcase of 'Sorbet Sky'. Just like Candy Cove, I designed this level with a focus on 4-step level design. In this level, the player has to jump between platforms and avoid the fireballs generated by the red boxes. When the player steps on a blue platform, it turns green, giving the player an extra challenge of turning all platforms green for a reward.
    ❮ ❯

    A Christmas Thing is a 3D Platformer conceptualised as a christmas present for the YouTube celebrity "raocow". The player takes control of Demo, a cycloptic girl, on her quest to find Super Leeks. The game spans two stages and a boss fight. It serves as a tech demo for a future Unity Engine framework and was developed over the course of three months.

    My role in this game was to design the hub and two platforming stages. In order to do so, I created a few textures and meshes and helped out with the game's programming by creating a script for moving platforms.
    H2Ω: Ohm's Lab (2017)
    Roles: Artist, Programmer, Level Designer
    Team size: 5 people
    1 / 3
    The title screen serves as an interactive tutorial. In order to play the game, the player has to perform a dash move through the glass to shatter it. He also has the opportunity to take out a defenseless enemy.
    2 / 3
    While moving upwards through the game's level, the player will encounter different types of enemies with unique behaviours. He will also need to keep an eye on his boost gauge, since without a boost he won't be able to cross the occasional glass barrier.
    3 / 3
    Water is an everpresent threat in this game. Move too slowly and it's game over.
    ❮ ❯

    H2Ω is a 2D Arcade Game about a security robot who finds his lab flooded and all other robots out of control. Now his goal is to defend himself against his former companions and escape the lab before the water catches up to him. It was developed over the course of five months.

    I was the lead programmer for this game, but also created art for the HUD and made sound effects. The game's stage was also laid out by me.
